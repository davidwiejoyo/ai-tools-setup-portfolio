# LinkedIn Organic Content Playbook (B2B SaaS)

---

## 1. Strategy Overview

### Target
Mid-level professionals who feel stuck in their career progression, with job seekers as a secondary audience.

These individuals are actively looking for practical ways to improve their positioning and move to the next level.

---

### Goal
Primary goal: Awareness (reach and visibility)  
Secondary goal: Authority (trust through actionable content)

---

### Positioning
Execution-first LinkedIn content for mid-level professionals, combining:
- Strong, direct hooks
- Structured, step-by-step insights
- Simple, easy-to-understand delivery

---

## 2. Content System

### Style

- Use strong, outcome-based hooks to capture attention  
  (source: Alex Hormozi, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/alexhormozi_people-clap-when-you-start-they-go-quiet-activity-7453835216814780416-D7uR)

- Use structured, step-by-step breakdowns to improve execution  
  (source: Austin Belcak, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/abelcak_9-ways-gpts-can-save-you-20-hours-week-activity-7453066651694198784-YchM)

- Use simple language to maximize clarity and usability  
  (source: Josh Spector, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thatjoshspector_things-to-remember-when-someone-turns-down-activity-7451662542839447552-o1Ir)

The goal is to deliver deep insights in a simple and actionable format.

---

### Content Pillars

1. Career Growth  
   - How to move from mid-level to senior roles  
   - Breaking stagnation in career progression  

2. Personal Positioning  
   - How to stand out on LinkedIn  
   - How to build perceived value professionally  

---

### Format

- Primarily text-based posts  
  (source: Alex Hormozi, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/alexhormozi_agree-activity-7454154411909586944-zj8r)

- Structured formats (lists, steps, breakdowns)  
  (source: Austin Belcak, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/abelcak_stop-uploading-your-resume-to-ai-every-time-activity-7452341875912515585-4_vd)

- Use visuals only when they improve clarity  
  (source: Dickie Bush, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/dickiebush_20m-offer-stacking-framework-with-ai-activity-7452333170487853057-ugWR)

---

### Frequency

- 3 core posts per week (deep, structured content)

- Optional reposts or short insights

Reason:
Consistency and clarity are prioritized over volume  
(source: Josh Spector, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thatjoshspector_things-to-remember-when-someone-turns-down-activity-7451662542839447552-o1Ir)

---

## 3. Post Structure SOP

### Hook

Use strong, direct hooks that:
- capture attention immediately  
- highlight a clear outcome  
- challenge assumptions  

(source: Alex Hormozi, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/alexhormozi_agree-activity-7453574087789969409-e4rH)

---

### Body

Structure content into:
- clear steps or points  
- logical progression  
- actionable insights  

Rules:
- prioritize clarity over complexity  
- break content into digestible chunks  

(source: Austin Belcak, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/abelcak_how-to-use-claude-cowork-to-find-new-jobs-activity-7452704263778107392-NwRr)

---

### CTA

CTA is used selectively.

Rules:
- MUST include CTA in tutorial-style posts  
- OPTIONAL in framework or insight posts  

Reason:
- tutorials require clear action  
- frameworks benefit from flexibility  

(source: Austin Belcak, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/abelcak_stop-uploading-your-resume-to-ai-every-time-activity-7452341875912515585-4_vd)

---

### Continuation

Posts may include continuation elements such as:
- teasing the next post  
- breaking topics into a series  

Purpose:
- improve retention  
- encourage execution over time  

(This is an original system based on observed patterns)

## 6. My Original Idea

Content Continuation System:

Each post is part of a sequence:
- Post 1 → introduce idea  
- Post 2 → expand  
- Post 3 → execute  

Reason:
Most content is consumed but not applied.

This system improves:
- retention  
- execution  
- engagement over time
